Alternance

An alternance program, also known as an apprenticeship or work-study program, is a type of educational program in France that combines classroom learning with practical work experience.

Advantage

Build a solid professional experience

Apprenticeship provides an extended immersion in the professional world, enabling students to quickly and effectively gain substantial practical experience, develop their skills, and build autonomy and professional confidence.

Study while being paid

Apprenticeship enables students to finance their education and living expenses through a salary, relieving financial burdens and allowing them to fully engage in their work and studies.

Work-study programs facilitate professional integration

Apprenticeship programs facilitate professional integration by offering concrete experience and sharpened skills, which is a major asset on a CV.

Inconvenient

An often intensive work rhythm

The work pace in an apprenticeship is often intense, requiring juggling between theoretical classes, work periods in the company, and homework, with a strong emphasis on organization, autonomy, and time management.

The difficulty of finding a company

Finding a Apprenticeship company can be difficult due to various factors such as the industry, the candidate's experience and the economic environment.

Strict regulations with Visa

Campus France is highly cautious when it comes to students pursuing an apprenticeship,
as any mishandling of the project could result in a significant risk of visa rejection.

For student outside France applying to Alternance

Two conditions to obtain a visa:

Finding the alternance before initiating the visa process is crucial, as the visa application will not be accepted without a confirmed alternance. Additionally, it is important to register the alternance with OPCO (Ministry of Labor), so it's essential to inform the company about this requirement.

Paying the school deposit. Upon arrival in France, you will have around 3 months to secure an alternance position. Failure to do so will result in reverting to the original program rhythm (rhythm initial) and being responsible for full tuition fees.

  • Know perfectly the company

To rock the interview, do thorough research on the company, learning about its products, culture, competitors, and news.
This will allow you to demonstrate your motivation and interest during the interview.

  • Speak with numbers

When researching the company, keep track of key figures such as the date it was founded, the number of employees or the turnover. This will allow you to answer the recruiter's questions and show your interest and involvement.

  • First impression

Be prepared to talk about your background, experience, skills and career plans in relation to the position you are applying for, highlighting your
suitability for the criteria in the ad. Be concise and convincing to capture the recruiter's attention.

  • Dress Professionally

During a work-study interview, unless it's in the banking, financial or insurance sector, don't hesitate to opt for comfortable clothes with a touch of elegance. Refer to photos of employees to get inspiration for the company's style of dress.

  • Prepare common interview questions

Be prepared to answer questions about your motivation and project achievements. Anticipate these questions and prepare solid answers to demonstrate your passion and commitment. Good preparation is essential for success.

  • How would you describe your soft skills and personality traits?

  • Could you please share your professional project or career goals?

  • What motivated you to choose a work-study program?

  • What aspects of this position's missions are particularly appealing to you?

  • What attracts you to the [sector/industry]?

  • What are your passions or areas of interest?

  • What is your understanding of the position and its future missions?
