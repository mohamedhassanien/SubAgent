How to write a successful cover letter

  • Pay attention to the presentation of your letter, work on the design and format.
  • Cite the name of the school and program several times to show that the letter is personalized.
  • Start your application early
  • Divide the letter into several parts, (see below)
  • Pay attention to spelling and use appropriate language: Spelling Correction: https://app.grammarly.com/ddocs/1229064852
  • Show that you know the program, cite the modules, certificates, accreditations etc.

Divide your cover letter into several parts

Introduction

Current situation and program choice

In the opening paragraph of your motivation letter, it is important to clearly state your current situation (student, professional, etc.) and express your enthusiasm by mentioning the program you are applying to.

Paragraph 1

Who you are and your experiences

In this section, you have the opportunity you want to speak about your relevant experiences, such as internships, work engagements, or involvement in organizations. Describe the valuable skills you have acquired from these experiences. Additionally, highlight your personal identity by sharing your interests, hobbies, and activities both within and outside of school.

Paragraph 2

Why are you interested in this school and program

This paragraph emphasizes the importance of showcasing meticulous motivation and thorough research. It highlights the attractive aspects of the school, including its vision, diverse international student community, state-of-the-art facilities, and renowned professors. It encourages explaining the specific interest in the chosen course, mentioning desired modules, proposed certifications, unique experiential learning opportunities, and exchange programs. Overall, it underscores the significance of demonstrating a well-informed and enthusiastic approach to the courses and the school.

Paragraph 3

Your professional project

After expressing your strong motivation to join this program, it is essential to articulate your professional ambitions and outline your aspirations for the future. Discuss your goals in both the short and long term, demonstrating a clear connection between your choice of studies and your career plans. Convince yourself and the admissions committee that this program is pivotal to the success of your project. You can elaborate on the specific company you aspire to join, the challenging assignments you seek, the industry sector that aligns with your interests, preferred locations, and other relevant factors that contribute to your career vision.

Conclusion

Reiterate motivation

The conclusion of your motivation letter should serve as a concise summary and reiterate your strong interest and suitability for the program. It should leave a lasting impression on the reader.
