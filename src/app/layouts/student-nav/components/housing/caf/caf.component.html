Caisse d’allocations familiales / aide personnalisée au logement

The APL (Aide personnalisée au logement) is a financial aid provided by the french government to reduce the amount of your rent (or your fees if you live in a hostel).

It is paid on the basis of your housing situation, regardless of your family situation: single, married, with or without any dependents.
The conditions of attribution differ depending on whether you are renting or living in a hostel.

CAF stands for Caisse d'Allocations Familiales, which is a French government agency that provides financial assistance to families and individuals.

CAF provides a range of financial assistance programs, including housing assistance, family allowances, childcare subsidies, and disability benefits.

To apply for CAF, you can visit the CAF website or go to a local CAF office. You will need to provide various documents, such as proof of identity, proof of address, and proof of income.

The processing time for CAF applications can vary depending on the program and the complexity of the application. However, applicants can generally expect to receive a response within a few weeks.

Yes, non-French citizens who meet the eligibility requirements can apply for CAF.

You can contact CAF by visiting their website, calling their toll-free number, or going to a local CAF office.
