What is the appartement inventory?

An appartment inventory is a document describing the condition of the property and its equipment. It must be drawn up when the tenant moves in, and attached to the rental contract. The equipment mentioned in the lease must appear in the inventory.This is referred to as the entry inventory of fixtures.

A new appartment inventory must also be drawn up when the tenant leaves. This is known as the exit inventory of fixtures.

FAQS

Apartment inventory is important because it provides a clear record of the condition of the rental property at the beginning of a tenancy. This can help prevent disputes between landlords and tenants at the end of the tenancy, particularly regarding the return of the security deposit.

In France, the landlord is typically responsible for creating the apartment inventory. However, tenants are usually given the opportunity to review and sign the inventory to confirm that they agree with its contents.

The apartment inventory should include all items in the rental property, such as furniture, appliances, and fixtures, along with their condition and any existing damage or wear and tear. It should also include the meter readings for gas, electricity, and water.

The apartment inventory should be created at the beginning of the tenancy, ideally before the tenant moves in. Both the landlord and the tenant should sign and date the inventory to confirm that they agree with its contents.

If there are disagreements about the apartment inventory, both the landlord and the tenant should try to resolve the issue through discussion and negotiation. If this is not possible, either party may need to seek legal advice or mediation.
