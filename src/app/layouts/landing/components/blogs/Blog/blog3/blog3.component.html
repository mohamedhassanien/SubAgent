Reasons to study in France

22 August, 2022

////////////////////////////////

France is the 3rd country in the world that welcomes the most international students. There are several reasons why students living abroad pursue and complete their studies in France.

Learn the language on the spot

To hope to become bilingual, you need to practice, and what better way than to be in France to speak and listen to the language. In practice there are many advantages to learning a new language, especially the language of Molière. French is the official language of nearly 200 million people, it is also the only language with English that is spoken on the 5 continents. Professionally, this facilitates integration into the hierarchy, since the more responsible you are in a position, the more likely you are to need a language other than your mother tongue, since communication with foreign entities is more common. On a personal level, in addition to the fact that speaking several languages is always a good idea, it can facilitate your travels and meetings with foreigners from other countries. And ideally, you can always move to the French-speaking country that has always attracted you. Once you learn the dialect, you will have privileged access to more than 47 countries in the world (If you’re not from the 46 countries listed above, then you’ve to follow a different procedure known as DAP )

The excellence of the educational system in France

Schools in France deliver internationally recognized high-level diplomas. A large and exceptional network of internationally renowned higher education institutions are at your disposal. Thus, a wide choice of programs is offered by schools and universities, at all levels and in different fields. France has always been renowned for its educational, scientific, industrial and economic know-how. As a result, it is at the origin of many great technological and industrial successes.

Tuition fees are much more affordable than in other countries, yet Europe is still a continent that cannot be ignored when it comes to student mobility, with 43% of young people going there to pursue their studies. The main host countries in Europe are:

France
Germany
The United Kingdom
Moreover, French higher education makes no distinction between French and foreign students.

A good quality of life, with, for example: an adequate public transportation network, a modern and accessible health care system at low cost for all, housing assistance granted to French and foreign nationals (under certain conditions), and a peaceful and safe daily life for all.

The wealth of French culture

Learning a new culture, such as French culture, must be very interesting. Although we hear European cultures are similar, each one keeps despite its own specificities. In France, the student experience is enriched through this dynamic culture, the artistic and cultural heritage, including festivals, theater, fashion, and gastronomy.

It is interesting to see other ways of life, other organizations. It gives you a more open-mindedness, and a more critical mind in all areas.

Career opportunities in the world's leading French companies

France also has international champions whose fame is spreading all over the world. Deezer: a global platform, the n°1 in France for music streaming, the platform has been able to reinvent the music market. For them, the state of mind and way of thinking comes before technical expertise. Deezer specializes in different areas of design and looks for ways to improve the user experience. Many positions are open such as, Software Engineers, Data Analysts, Business Developers, Copyright Accountants...


LVMH: between creativity and innovation, the Group is the only player present simultaneously in five major luxury sectors: Wines & Spirits, Fashion & Leather Goods, Perfumes & Cosmetics, Watches & Jewelry and Selective Retailing. LVMH represents a very great opportunity for those who dream of entering the luxury sector, thanks to their diverse activities.


Carrefour: a French group, has been a pioneer in the hypermarket concept since 1963. Carrefour is committed to the professional future of young people affected by the crisis and has announced a proactive and inclusive recruitment plan for the year 2021. Their goal is to recruit 15,000 young people in 2021.


Total: a French oil and gas company, is the fifth of the six largest "super majors" in the sector worldwide. What better way to start one's career than with a quality experience with an international group, because Total is strengthening its commitment to young people. Several advantages are open to you with TOTAL, attractive remuneration policy, employee shareholding, international mobility ... Moreover, 500 jobs to achieve at Total.


Orange: a public company of the France Telecom Group, is made up of several subsidiaries (French or international). Orange offers graduate programs, if you finish your master 2 and you have less than 18 months of experience (excluding internship and apprenticeship). You could exercise your talent abroad thanks to international work experience with a total of 1,600 candidates in the graduate program, 50% of whom are women.


Airbus: is a European, French aircraft manufacturer, the group is involved in space launchers, artificial satellites, missiles, fighter aircraft. Airbus innovates in autonomy and connectivity, and zero emissions. For those concerned with environmental and societal responsibility, Airbus can be a source of fulfillment for them.
